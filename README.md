📊 RFM Customer Segmentation & Analytics Dashboard

📝 Project Overview
This project delivers an end-to-end data analytics solution to identify customer purchasing patterns and drive retention strategies. Using the RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) model, I transformed raw e-commerce transactional data into actionable business segments.
The goal is to move beyond "total sales" and understand who the customers are, how often they shop, and when they are at risk of churning.

🛠️ Tech Stack & Tools
SQL: Data cleaning, transformation, and RFM score calculation.
Excel: Initial data exploration and schema validation.
Power BI: Data modeling, DAX measures, and interactive dashboarding.

🚀 Project Workflow1. 
Data Extraction & Cleaning (SQL)----
Before analysis, I performed rigorous data cleaning to ensure high data quality:
Handling Missing Values: Removed records with null CustomerIDs.
Data Integrity: Filtered out cancelled transactions and corrected negative quantities.
Formatting: Standardized date formats and calculated TotalSales (Quantity × UnitPrice) at the row level.

RFM Calculation & Segmentation (SQL)Using CTEs (Common Table Expressions) and Window Functions, I aggregated data at the customer level:
   Recency: Calculated as $DaysSinceLastPurchase$ relative to the dataset's max date.
   Frequency: Count of unique InvoiceNo per customer.Monetary: Total revenue generated by the customer.
   Scoring: Assigned 1-5 scores using NTILE() or custom CASE statements to rank customers.
   Segmentation: Grouped customers into categories:
   Champions, Loyal Customers, Potential Loyalists, At Risk, and Lost.
   
Data Visualization & Modeling (Power BI)---
I imported the processed SQL views into Power BI to build a dynamic analytical tool:
   Data Modeling: Established relationships between the RFM results and dimensional tables (e.g., Recency_Dim).
   DAX Measures: Created custom measures for Average Frequency, Total Revenue ($8.70M), and Customer Count %.Interactive Visuals:
      Treemaps: To visualize the volume of customers in each segment (highlighting the "Others" and "At Risk" groups).
      Bar Charts: Comparing revenue contribution vs. customer volume per segment.
      Slicers: Added "Customer Segments" buttons for deep-dive filtering.
   UI/UX Design: Implemented a dark-themed, professional layout with a focus on KPI clarity.
   
📈 Key Business Insights
   Revenue Concentration: Identified that the "Others" segment contributes the highest revenue ($5.0M), suggesting a large base of one-time or irregular shoppers.
   Churn Risk: Flagged $0.5M in revenue currently held by "At Risk" customers, providing a clear target for re-engagement campaigns.
   Performance Metrics: Average shopping frequency across the base sits at 4.27, setting a benchmark for future loyalty programs.
